About Morning Cup AI

Warm, plain-English guidance for the adults AI left behind in its own excitement

Picture two friends catching up over a morning cup of coffee — one of them just learned something useful about AI, and they're sharing it. Not lecturing. Not showing off. Just explaining it the way you'd want someone to explain it to you, before the day gets going.

That's what this newsletter is.

Who it's for

Morning Cup AI is written for adults 55 and older who are curious about artificial intelligence but feel left out of the conversation. The technology news is too fast, too jargon-heavy, and too focused on the people who already understand it. You're not the target audience there — but you are here.

Whether you're a retired teacher who wants to use AI to write a memoir, a grandparent who wants to understand what your grandkids are talking about, or someone who just heard the term "ChatGPT" for the first time and wants to know if it's relevant to your life — you belong here.

The morning cup idea

A morning cup of coffee is one of life's small anchors — a quiet moment before the noise kicks in. It's when you read, think, and catch up with the people you trust. That's the spirit Morning Cup AI tries to match: something worth a few minutes of your morning, delivered without fuss.

Not the gleaming tech-conference version of AI, but the practical, honest, sometimes-messy reality of figuring out whether these tools are actually useful for your life. And whether they are.

What you'll get

Every week, one focused, readable email. It might explain what a particular AI tool actually does. It might walk through a specific task step by step. It might warn you about something that looks helpful but isn't. It might just be an encouraging reminder that you're not behind — you're learning.

No pop quizzes. No homework. No jargon without explanation. And no breathless hype about how AI will change everything by Tuesday.

No hidden agenda

Morning Cup AI is independent. We don't take money from AI companies. We don't earn commissions on the tools we mention. If we recommend something, it's because we genuinely think it will help you — and if we think something is risky or overhyped, we'll say that too.
