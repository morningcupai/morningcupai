How to use AI to find and cancel subscriptions you forgot you were paying for.

A simple audit that takes 20 minutes — and could save you hundreds of dollars a year.

Money & Retirement · February 10, 2026 · 3 min read

In this article:

  1. The subscription problem most people have
  2. How AI helps you find them
  3. Step-by-step: running your audit
  4. How to cancel what you find
  5. This week's AI prompt
  6. Scam watch

Most people are paying for at least one thing they completely forgot about.

A streaming service from two years ago. A news site you signed up for during an election. A fitness app you used for three weeks. A free trial that quietly became a monthly charge.

It adds up faster than you think.

The Subscription Problem Most People Have

The average American pays for 4.5 subscription services they no longer actively use.

That is often $50 to $150 per month leaving your account quietly — without you ever making a conscious decision to keep paying.

The companies that offer subscriptions are very good at making cancellation hard and renewal automatic.

Most people only discover forgotten subscriptions when they carefully read their bank statement — which most people never do line by line.

How AI Helps You Find Them

You do not need to go through your bank statement alone.

AI can help you build a systematic checklist, identify the kinds of charges to look for, and even draft cancellation emails for services that make it difficult to quit.

Think of it as having a patient, organized friend sit down with you and go through everything together.

It is not complicated. It just requires 20 focused minutes and a copy of your last two months of bank or credit card statements.

Step-by-Step — Running Your Audit

  1. Download or print your last two months of bank and credit card statements
  2. Open ChatGPT or Claude
  3. Tell the AI you want to do a subscription audit and paste in a list of every charge you do not immediately recognize
  4. Ask the AI to help you identify which ones are likely subscription services
  5. For each one — decide: keep, cancel, or investigate further
  6. For anything you want to cancel, ask the AI to draft a cancellation email or find the cancellation steps

Pay special attention to charges between $5 and $25 per month.

These are small enough to ignore month after month but large enough to add up to hundreds of dollars over a year.

What to Look For Specifically

  • Streaming services — Netflix, Hulu, Disney+, Paramount+, Apple TV+, HBO Max
  • News and magazine subscriptions
  • Cloud storage — iCloud, Google One, Dropbox
  • Music services — Spotify, Apple Music, Pandora
  • Fitness and wellness apps
  • VPN services
  • Password managers you forgot you had
  • Charity donations set to auto-renew
  • Amazon Prime and similar membership programs
  • Any charge from a company ending in ".io" or with an unfamiliar name

How to Cancel What You Find

Some services make cancellation easy — one click in the app or website settings.

Others are deliberately difficult — buried menus, mandatory phone calls, or confusing cancellation flows designed to frustrate you into giving up.

If you cannot figure out how to cancel something, ask the AI directly.

Type: "How do I cancel my [service name] subscription?" and it will walk you through the exact steps.

If a company refuses to cancel or charges you after you cancelled, you can contact your bank and dispute the charge as unauthorized.

You have more power than most companies want you to know.

Quick Check

See a charge you do not recognize? Use this right now:

I see a charge on my bank statement for [amount] from a company called [name]. I do not remember signing up for anything from them. Can you tell me what this company is and how I would go about cancelling or disputing this charge?
This Week's AI Prompt

Set aside 20 minutes, pull up your last two months of statements, and paste this into ChatGPT or Claude:

I want to do a full subscription audit to find services I am paying for but no longer using. Here are all the charges from my bank and credit card statements that I do not immediately recognize: [paste your list of charges here — company name and monthly amount] Please help me: 1. Identify which of these are likely subscription services 2. Flag any that seem unusually high or suspicious 3. Suggest which ones are worth keeping versus cancelling based on common usage 4. Draft a short cancellation email I can send for any service I decide to cancel Keep your explanations simple and practical — I just want to stop paying for things I am not using.

This Week's Scam Watch

A growing scam targets people who have recently cancelled subscriptions. Scammers send official-looking emails or texts claiming your cancellation was not processed and you will be charged unless you call a number or click a link immediately.

Legitimate companies confirm cancellations by email automatically — they do not threaten you or demand you call to verify. If you receive a message like this after cancelling a service, do not click any links. Go directly to the company's official website to check your account status.

Twenty minutes and a copy of your bank statement.

That is all it takes to potentially save yourself hundreds of dollars every year.

AI makes the process faster, less overwhelming, and more thorough than doing it alone.

Run your audit this week. You may be surprised what you find.
