Scam Protection

Your grandson called. He needed money. But it wasn't him.

How AI voice cloning scams work — and the one thing that stops them every time.

January 7, 2026 2 min read

The phone rings. It sounds exactly like your grandson — panicked, scared, saying he's been in an accident and needs you to wire money right away. The breathing, the way he says your name, even the little catch in his voice when he's upset. You almost reach for your wallet. But here's the truth: it wasn't him. It was an AI. And this is happening to real families every single day.

Voice cloning technology has gotten frighteningly good. A scammer only needs three to ten seconds of someone's voice — pulled from a birthday video on Facebook, a voicemail, or even a TikTok — to create a clone that sounds identical to your loved one. They call you pretending that person is in a crisis, counting on fear and love to make you act before you think.

This is called a voice cloning scam. It is one of the fastest growing scams targeting older adults in America right now. The FBI received over 40,000 complaints about it last year alone. Real families have lost tens of thousands of dollars in a single phone call.

Here is exactly what to watch for. The call always comes with urgency — there is always a reason you need to act right now and cannot wait. They ask for wire transfers, gift cards, or cash, never a check or credit card because those can be reversed. They tell you not to tell anyone else in the family. And no matter how real the voice sounds, something feels slightly off — the emotion is there but the conversation feels a little too scripted.

The single most effective defense costs nothing and takes two minutes to set up. Create a family code word. Sit down with your children and grandchildren this week and agree on a random word that only your family knows. Something like pineapple, blue door, or grandma's kitchen. If you ever get a distressing call from a family member, ask them for the code word. A real family member will know it instantly. An AI scammer never will.

The second defense is even simpler — hang up and call back. No matter how urgent it sounds, hang up and dial your family member's real number yourself. If they are truly in an emergency they will answer. If the call was a scam you just saved yourself everything. Set up that code word this week. It takes two minutes and it could save you everything.

This Week's Scam Watch

Scammers are now using AI to clone the voices of doctors and Medicare representatives, calling older adults to say their coverage is being suspended and demanding immediate payment to restore it. Medicare will never call you unexpectedly asking for payment. If you receive a call like this, hang up immediately and call Medicare directly at 1-800-MEDICARE to verify.

You are not naive for almost falling for this — these scams are designed by professionals to bypass your instincts. Knowing they exist is already your biggest protection.
